## LTX-Video Evaluation (2024)

### Human Preference Survey

**Setup:**
- 20 participants divided into two groups of 10
- 1,000 prompts for text-to-video
- 1,000 image+prompt pairs for image-to-video (images generated by FLUX.1)
- 5-second videos at 768x512 resolution
- All models: 40 diffusion steps
- Random ordering of video pairs from random model pairs
- Model names hidden from participants
- "Skip" option for uncertain comparisons
- Win rate calculated as: wins / (wins + losses)
- Inter-group consistency: <2% difference in win rates

**Models Compared (similar scale ~2B):**
- LTX-Video
- Open-Sora Plan
- CogVideoX (2B)
- PyramidFlow

**Results:**
- LTX-Video **significantly outperforms** all compared models in both text-to-video and image-to-video
- Full pairwise win ratio matrices provided (all model pairs evaluated, not just LTX-Video vs others)

### Speed Benchmarks
- **2 seconds** for 121 frames at 768x512, 20 diffusion steps on H100
- Faster-than-real-time: generates 5 seconds of 24fps video in 2 seconds
- Fastest model of its kind at release

### Ablation Results

**Reconstruction GAN vs Traditional GAN:**
- At 1:192 compression, standard GAN losses produce visible artifacts (especially in high-motion + detail)
- rGAN significantly reduces artifacts

**RoPE Frequency Spacing:**
- Exponential: consistently lower training loss
- Inverse-exponential: higher loss throughout training

**Denoising VAE Decoder:**
- With denoising (t=0.05): strongly preferred in internal user study
- Without denoising (t=0.0): more artifacts, especially in high-motion videos

## LTX-2 Evaluation (2025)

### Audiovisual Quality
- **Human preference studies** comparing to open-source and proprietary systems
- Participants evaluated: visual realism, audio fidelity, temporal synchronization (lip-sync, foley)
- **Significantly outperforms** open-source alternatives (Ovi)
- **Comparable to** leading proprietary models (Veo 3, Sora 2)

### Video-Only Benchmarks
**Artificial Analysis Public Rankings (November 2025):**
- **3rd in Image-to-Video** generation
- **4th in Text-to-Video** generation
- Surpassed: Sora 2 Pro, Wan 2.2-14B
- Joint audio-video training does NOT compromise visual quality

### Inference Speed Comparison (H100, 121 frames @ 720p, single Euler step, CFG=1)

| Model | Parameters | Modalities | Relative Speed |
|-------|-----------|------------|----------------|
| LTX-2 | 19B | Audio+Video | 1x (baseline) |
| Wan 2.2 | 14B | Video only | ~18x slower |
| Ovi | 2x5B | Audio+Video | Slower than LTX-2 |

- Performance gap widens at higher resolutions and longer durations
- Due to optimized latent space (1:192 compression from LTX-Video heritage)

### Temporal Scope Comparison

| Model | Max Duration | Type |
|-------|-------------|------|
| LTX-2 | 20 seconds | Open-source |
| Sora 2 | 16 seconds | Proprietary |
| Veo 3 | 12 seconds | Proprietary |
| Ovi | 10 seconds | Open-source |
| Wan 2.5 | 10 seconds | Open-source |

## AVControl Evaluation (2026)

### VACE Benchmark Results
- **Outperforms all evaluated baselines** on:
  - Depth-guided generation
  - Pose-guided generation
  - Inpainting
  - Outpainting
- **Competitive results** on camera control

### Audio-Visual Benchmarks
Audio intensity control comparison:

|--------|------------|------------|-----------|-----|----------------|
| CAFA | 12.60 | 2.02 | 13.45 | 0.21 | 200K |
| AVControl | Competitive | Competitive | Competitive | Competitive | ~4K |

### Efficiency Metrics
- Total training: ~55K steps across ALL 13 modalities
- vs VACE: 200K steps for single monolithic model
- Individual modalities converge in 1,000-3,000 steps
- VBench depth scores: 81.1 @ 1K steps, 81.6 @ 3K steps

### LoRA Rank Ablation
- Rank 32: VBench 80.9
- Rank 64: VBench 81.3
- Rank 128: VBench 81.6
- Spread < 1 point; rank 128 used as default

## Evaluation Methodology Notes

### LTX-Video Evaluation
- Focus on human preference (no automatic metrics like VBench reported in paper)
- Evaluation criteria: visual quality, motion fidelity, prompt adherence
- All model pairs compared (not just LTX-Video vs others)

### LTX-2 Evaluation
- Human preference studies for audiovisual quality
- Third-party rankings (Artificial Analysis) for video-only benchmarks
- Runtime benchmarks on H100 hardware

### Limitations of Evaluation
- LTX-Video paper did not report VBench or FVD scores (human evaluation only)
- Comparison limited to similar-scale models (~2B parameters for LTX-Video)
- LTX-2 compared to both open-source and proprietary models
