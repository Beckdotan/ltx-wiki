## Overview

The Lightricks/LTX-Video repository has approximately 960 forks. Most forks are simple clones without significant modifications, but several community derivatives add meaningful functionality.

---

## Notable Derivative Repositories

### 1. LTX-2-desktop/LTX-2.3

> **URL:** <https://github.com/LTX-2-desktop/LTX-2.3>

An unofficial community packaging of LTX-2.3 that emphasizes:
- "Commercial-grade generation quality on par with Google Veo 3"
- No censorship or content restrictions
- Dynamic CPU offloading and 8-bit/4-bit quantization
- Designed to run on mid-range PCs

### 2. KONAKONA666/LTX-Video

> **URL:** <https://github.com/KONAKONA666/LTX-Video>

LTXVideo Q8 -- a community fork focusing on FP8 quantized inference.

### 3. y2k7380/ltx-video

> **URL:** <https://github.com/y2k7380/ltx-video>

Standard fork of the official repository.

### 4. t1seungy/ltx-video

> **URL:** <https://github.com/t1seungy/ltx-video>

Standard fork of the official repository.

### 5. sayakpaul/q8-ltx-video

> **URL:** <https://github.com/sayakpaul/q8-ltx-video>

Shows how to use Q8 kernels with HuggingFace `diffusers` to optimize inference of LTX-Video on ADA GPUs. Notable as a community guide/tutorial repo.

## Awesome List

### wildminder/awesome-ltx2

> **URL:** <https://github.com/wildminder/awesome-ltx2>
> **Stars:** 286 | **Forks:** 23 | **Last updated:** April 13, 2026

A curated list of models, text encoders, and tools for LTX-2. Covers:
- All model variants (LTX-2.3, LTX-2 19B) with sizes and quantization options
- GGUF quantizations (Q2_K through Q4_K, 12-18 GB range)
- LoRA adapters (distilled, ranks 105-384)
- Spatial and temporal upscalers
- Community tools like LTX2.3-Multifunctional
- Prompting guide and basics documentation
