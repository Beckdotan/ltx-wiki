# LTX Video Spatial Upscaler

The LTX Video Spatial Upscaler is a diffusion-based model that enhances the spatial resolution of videos generated by LTX Video models. It operates in latent space rather than pixel space, upscaling latent representations by 2x on both height and width dimensions.

Introduced alongside [[ltx-video-097|LTX Video v0.9.7]], it complements the built-in [[multiscale-rendering]] approach for users who need even higher resolution output. Updated versions are available for [[ltx-video-098|v0.9.8]].

## Architecture

- **Model type:** Latent Diffusion Video Spatial Upscaler
- **Developer:** Lightricks
- **Input:** Latent video frames from an LTX Video model
- **Output:** Higher-resolution latent video frames
- **Upscaling factor:** 2x on both height and width
- **Operates in:** Latent space (not pixel space) for efficiency
- **VAE sharing:** Shares the VAE with the base generation model

## Compatibility

- `Lightricks/LTX-Video-0.9.7-dev`
- `Lightricks/LTX-Video-0.9.7-distilled`
- 0.9.8 variants (updated upscaler available)

## Constraints

- **Resolution:** Works best under 720x1280
- **Resolution divisibility:** Must be divisible by 32
- **Frame count:** Divisible by 8 + 1 (e.g., 257 frames max)
- **Auto-padding:** Non-compliant inputs are padded with -1, then cropped after upscaling

## Recommended Workflow

The spatial upscaler works best in a multi-step pipeline:

### Step 1: Generate at Lower Resolution

Generate video at reduced resolution, outputting latents (not decoded pixels).

### Step 2: Upscale Latents

Pass the latents through `LTXLatentUpsamplePipeline` for 2x spatial upscaling.

### Step 3: Denoise Upscaled Video (Recommended)

Run an additional denoising pass on the upscaled latents with `denoise_strength=0.4`. This preserves structure while refining details and significantly improves quality.

### Step 4: Downscale to Expected Resolution

If the upscaled resolution exceeds the target, resize frames to the expected output size.

## Key Design Decisions

- Operating in latent space rather than pixel space makes the upscaler significantly more efficient
- The optional denoising pass after upscaling improves quality substantially -- `denoise_strength=0.4` is the recommended balance between structure preservation and detail refinement
- VAE tiling can be enabled (`pipe.vae.enable_tiling()`) for memory efficiency during decoding

## Relationship to Multiscale Rendering

The spatial upscaler is distinct from (but complementary to) the [[multiscale-rendering]] built into the 13B model:

- **Multiscale rendering** is the internal generation process (draft low-res, refine to high-res)
- **Spatial upscaler** is an additional post-processing step for further 2x upscaling beyond the built-in rendering
- Typical high-quality workflow: Multiscale render at base resolution -> spatial upscale 2x -> optional refinement pass

## Diffusers API

The upscaler uses `LTXLatentUpsamplePipeline` from the Diffusers library:

```python
from diffusers import LTXConditionPipeline, LTXLatentUpsamplePipeline

pipe_upsample = LTXLatentUpsamplePipeline.from_pretrained(
    "Lightricks/ltxv-spatial-upscaler-0.9.7",
    vae=pipe.vae,
    torch_dtype=torch.bfloat16
)
```
